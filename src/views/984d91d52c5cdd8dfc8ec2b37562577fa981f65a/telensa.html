Telensa

Redesigning the world's number one wireless outdoor lighting control.

The brief

We were asked by Telensa to rework the UI of their street lighting control application. This is used by councils globally to control their street lighting, with the aim on eventually becoming a ‘smart city’ application. As the scope was so large, our MVP was to look at one aspect of the application which was daily fault management.

Discovery

I worked as the sole designer on the project and began my journey by visiting users of the old system to see what tasks they need to complete and how they achieved them using the old system. This helped me focus on not only on the tasks that they needed to carry out, but also how to simplify the process for them.

After visiting the users of the system, I was in a much better place to have some real input into the user story mapping session. This is a big product, and because of that, a lot of internal stakeholders came along for the user story mapping session. But between us we nailed down the core functionality for the fault finding aspect of the product.

Design

Wireframes

Using the user stories and personas as a guide at all times, I worked up wireframes to test on internal users and also users at councils in Essex, Leicester and Ipswich. The initial wireframes covered viewing a triage of any fults on a given day, and then digging deeper into that fault to enable diagnosis.

Using their feedback, I was able to quickly amend the wireframes and then move on to create my grid system and style guide.

High fidelity design

The final design uses Google’s material design as this application is used globally, I wanted to UI to be as easy to use, and modern as possible.

I'm now beginning the pricess again to look at another aspect of the product.
