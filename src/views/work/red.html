RED

A real-estate database for landlords

The brief

The Rehau Group is a family-owned polymer business, which works in the fields of construction, industry and as an automotive supplier. They have over 20,000 employees at over 170 locations around the world and the turnover of the whole group amounts to approximately €2.8 billion.

As a consequence, they hold a large portfolio of real estate which has been difficult to manage due to a lack of a centralised property management strategy. That’s where we came in, to create a responsive real-estate database.

Discovery

We flew in the key stakeholders from the client side, to get everyone in room and map out what we wanted the product to be. Their current ‘system’ was to have everything paper based, in office all over the world. When asked how many proprties they owned, the answer took a year to find. So we knew that a key MVP was to have a place where all of the properties are stored, and that they can see at a glance what the total number is.

We then broke down the MVP into manageable chunks of work, which would be delivered continuously.

Design

Sketching session

After writing up our user stories, acceptance criteria, and feature files together with the client, we were ready to begin with our sketching sessions. These are small workshops I’ll run with the client Product Owner and our product team (frontend and backend developers) and of course, myself. When these are scheduled, I’ll ask all participants to carry out some design research so that we’re fully inspired when we go into the sketching.

Once we’ve shared any inspirational solutions with the group, we’ll use Trello as a guide to begin sketching out some really quick solutions to our problems.

We use a trick from the Design Sprint called ‘crazy eights’ which involves folding the paper into eight sections to sketch out multiple ideas. Encouraging people to think beyond their initial preconceptions more often than not brings out some of the best ideas.

Once we’re finished sketching, everyone gets the chance to explain their ideas before voting on their favourite concept. Once we have a idea of the strongest concepts we have everything we need to mock up vanilla wireframes ready for the first round of critique.

High fidelity design

Unfortunately, as this client were based in Germany, and trusting a consultancy firm to liaise with us, we never got access to real users for our user testing, but we certainly carried out testing within the company to sense check our user journeys.

Once everyone was happy, I was able to add in the final design treatment based on the company’s already established branding and colour palette.

Development

I work closely with our developers and I’m always at hand when the inevitable questions and queries come up ‘what happens when a user clicks X’ etc. Once they're finished, I'll run through a detailed quality assurance process when the release goes onto staging, so we can make any necessary changes before final sign off with the client.

Then the whole process happens all over again for the next iteration of work
