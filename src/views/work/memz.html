MEMZ

Redesigning a real-time photo streaming app

Why?

MEMZ was initially made in a hack day where we chose to create a live photosharing app. When we decided to take the product seriously, it looked like this:

Ok, so it’s not the worst, but I didn’t design it, so I asked if we could take it back to the drawing board.

Discovery

We worked together to think of a use case for the app and decided on a big life event. A wedding was too obvious (as that was where the initial idea came from) so we chose a hen party. From there, it was easy to write up our user personas.

Then we walked through the journey of the most important user, Rachel. This led us to our MVP, and from there we chose the least cards that would deliver value for Rachel.

Design

As this was a redesign of a product, and also a hack week, I went straight into simplifying the design and creating a clean interface. My first step was to simplify the logo. Then I worked with the team (our target users) to create a user friendly and intuitive sign up process.

Then came the feed itself, which ended up as a really clean interface to let the photos shine.
